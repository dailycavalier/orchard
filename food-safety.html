Food Safety

Food safety is a primary focus at Wood’s Produce Company. We understand that food safety is everyone’s responsibility and we ensure that food safety is a priority in every step of the supply chain to prevent the risk of contamination.

We require our suppliers to be third party audited in accordance with Global Food Safety Initiative (GFSI) benchmarks and that our product is shipped on refrigerated trucks to prevent breaking the cold-chain. Within our facility, we have established rigorous food handling procedures, strict security measures to prevent bio-terrorism, and comprehensive sanitation procedures to ensure. Our facilities have achieved Safe Quality Food Institute Level 2000, USDA Good Handling & Agricultural Practices, and USDA Preventative Food Defense Procedures certifications.

Some of the food safety programs and procedures in place at Wood’s Produce Company:

  • Every employee is trained extensively in safe food handling and sanitation procedures
  • 9 independent coolers with varying temperature and humidity levels to store product in ideal conditions
  • Full Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point (HACCP) Program
  • Ability to conduct full emergency recalls within hours of notification
  • 24-hour video surveillance of the interior and exterior facilities to prevent unauthorized handling of product
  • Fleet of refrigerated trucks maintain the cold-chain from the warehouse to the customer’s door
  • Use of all electric warehouse equipment to prevent dangerous fumes from coming in contact with product
  • 4500 Kilowatt generator to maintain our cold-chain through severe weather conditions

  • SQF Institute Level 2000

  • Accredited HACCP Based Food Safety Plans

  • USDA Good Handling & Agricultural Practices

  • USDA Preventative Food Defense Procedures
